What are your trading hours?

Our clients are able to trade from market open on Monday to market close on Friday, 24 hours a day 5 days a week. Our specific opening hours are different across the world due to time zone differences. As such we have our opening time in the three major markets.


What Spreads do you offer?

For an ECN Standard Account, the spreads start from 0.8 pips
For an ECN Prime Account, the spreads start from 0.1 pips
For an ECN Institutional Account, the spreads start from 0.0 pips


What is your commission rate?

For an ECN Standard Account, there is no commission
For an ECN Prime Account, the commission is USD 6.00 per lot (charged on the opening of the trade)
For an ECN Institutional Account commission on this Account is negotiable


Does you use a Dealing Desk?

No, BlackBull Markets is a strictly non-dealing desk. We only offer ECN Accounts and Straight Through Processing services which both prevent this practice and conflicts of interest.


Does slippage occur on your server?

Slippage almost never occurs, however, this being said, during very high trading periods (such as during important news events) slippage can occur. Slippage may be to both your benefit or your detriment.


Do you do re-quotes?

No, BlackBull Markets does not do re-quotes


What Platforms do you offer?

We have a BlackBull Markets MetaTrader 4, MetaTrader 5 and TradingView as our primary trading platforms. We have made this available for clients on Windows, Mac, Android, and iOS.

We also offer access to the WebTrader Platform, so our clients can access our markets wherever they can find an internet connection.


What are your Margin Calls?

When your margin drops below 70%, you will receive a margin call notification to top up your funds within the MT4 or MT5 platform. Once your margin drops below 50% your trade will be automatically closed out.


Do you have Swap-Free Accounts?

Yes, BlackBull Markets offers swap-free compliant accounts.

In trading, swaps are like adjustments made to your trade when it’s held overnight. These swaps can be classified as double swaps or triple swaps.


A double swap represents the typical adjustment made each night for holding a trade, which can either be in your favor or against you, depending on the interest rate differential.


Conversely, triple swaps are a unique case that occurs on Wednesday nights. When we talk about triple swaps, it means traders are subject to a slightly more extensive adjustment that accounts for three days: Wednesday night, Thursday night, and Friday night. This extended adjustment is primarily to include the interest over the weekend when trading markets are typically closed. It, too, can be either positive or negative.


The triple swap is calculated by multiplying the standard daily swap rate by three.

Here is an example of how a triple swap might work:


  • Let’s say you open a long position on EUR/USD on Wednesday at 1.1000.

  • The standard daily swap rate for EUR/USD is -0.1 pips.

  • Therefore, if you hold your position open overnight on Wednesday, you will be charged a triple swap of -0.3 pips.

  • This means that your position will be debited or credited with $300 per lot that you are trading.

If you are planning to hold a position open over the weekend, be sure to factor in the triple swap charge into your trading plan.
