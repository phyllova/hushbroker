Setting a Stop Loss (SL) and Take Profit (TP) in trading is crucial for risk mitigation and should be included in most trading strategies. It is important to always consider SL and TP in every position.


Setting a stop loss and take profit in MT5 can be done in the new order window while opening a new position, or after a trade has already been opened. 

Set a SL and TP for a new trade

  • Open the New Order window to place a new trade. 
  • Input your predetermined SL and TP prices in their respective fields. 
  • Input your volume size and then place your order. 
 

Set a SL and TP for an existing trade

Setting a Stop Loss and Take Profit for an existing trade in MT5 can be done in 2 ways: 

  1. By double clicking on a trade in the trade window and modifying the stop loss and take profit in their respective fields
  1. By dragging the stop loss and take profit levels on the chart directly 
 

Using the Trade tab 

Navigate to the trade tab to reveal all the trades you have open, including the sizes, stop loss, take profit, and entry price for every trade. 

In rare cases where the Trade tab is nowhere to be found, press CTRL + t to open it. 

From here on: 

  • Hover over the trade to be modified and right-click 
  • Select “Modify or Delete” 
 


After the order window appears, fill the new stop loss or take profit in their respective fields and then click “modify…”  



You will then see the SL and TP levels you have set in the Trade tab. 


Using the chart

Instead of using the Trade tab, it is possible to set and modify the SL or TP levels to the desired position directly on the chart.  

  • To set a SL and TP, click the dotted line on the chart that designates your entry position and drag the SL and TP to the correct level.
 

 
  • In the case of the illustration above, you can see the position on chart “SELL 0.5 at 0.67222”.
  • If you drag up on a Sell order a SL will be set and if you drag downwards a TP will be set, and vice versa for a Buy order.
  • You can then proceed to modify the SL and TP on the chart by clicking the dragging the TP and SL red dotted lines if you so wish.

By changing the timeframe on MT5, traders can gain valuable insights into price movements, identify trends, and make informed trading decisions. In this article, we will explore the simple steps to change timeframes on MT5, allowing traders to optimize their trading strategies and enhance their overall trading experience. 

 

You first need to launch the platform and open a chart of the financial instrument you wish to analyze. You can do this by selecting the desired asset from the “Market Watch” window and then right-clicking on it. From the context menu, choose “Chart Window” to open a new chart for the selected instrument. 

 

 

Once you have the chart open, you will find the timeframe options located at the top of the chart window. By default, MT5 displays the timeframe in minutes, ranging from 1 minute (M1) to 1 month (MN1). The available timeframe options include M1, M5, M15, M30, H1, H4, D1, W1, and MN1. 

 

Select the desired timeframe from the list by clicking on it. Instantly, the chart will refresh and display the price data based on your chosen timeframe. 

 

 

Apart from the standard timeframe options, MT5 also has some hidden timeframes. These timeframes can be found in the top menu under “Charts -> Timeframes”. Here you can find more minute and hour timeframes.

One essential feature of MT5 is the ability to view their trade history. Analyzing your past trades can provide valuable insights into your trading performance, helping you make better decisions in the future. In this article, we’ll walk you through the step-by-step process of accessing and viewing your trade history in MT5. 

 

To access your trade history, first, launch your MT5 trading platform and log into your trading account. Once the platform is open, locate the “Toolbox” window at the bottom of the screen. If the terminal is not visible, you can enable it by pressing “Ctrl + T” on your keyboard or navigating to the “View” menu and selecting “Toolbox.” 

 

 

Within the “Terminal” window, you’ll find several tabs at the bottom. Click on the “History” tab to view your trade history. This tab displays a list of all the closed trades associated with your trading account. 

 

 

Once you click on the “History” tab, you’ll see a list of your closed trades, including important details such as the trade’s entry and exit points, trade duration, trade size, profit or loss, and the date and time the trade was closed. By default, the trades will be displayed in reverse chronological order, with the most recent trade appearing at the top. 

 

 

MT5 allows you to customize the display of your trade history to suit your preferences. You can change the order of the trades, apply filters based on various criteria like time range or instrument, and export the trade history to a spreadsheet for further analysis. Additionally, you can modify the columns displayed to include or exclude specific information. All of these options can be accessed by right clicking within the window and selecting/deselecting the column variables.

One crucial aspect of forex trading is understanding and managing roll over or swap rates. In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to calculate and view roll over/swap rates within the MT5 platform. By mastering this process, traders can make informed decisions regarding their positions and effectively manage their trading costs. 

 

Locate the “Market Watch” panel. If it is not visible, go to the “View” menu and select “Market Watch” or use the shortcut key combination “Ctrl + M” to open it.

 

 

In the Market Watch window, right-click and select “Symbols” from the drop-down menu. Alternatively, you can directly use the shortcut key combination “Ctrl + U” to access the Symbols window. 

 

 

Choose the currency pair you want to view the roll over/swap rates for and click on it. This action will open the Symbol Specification window.

 

 

Within the Symbol Specification window, scroll down to the “Swap” information. Here, you will find the roll over/swap rates for both long (buy) and short (sell) positions. The Swap tab will display the roll over/swap rates in points or pips.

 

One important thing to note is that the rates may be presented in positive or negative values. Positive swap rates indicate that you will receive interest for holding the position overnight, while negative swap rates mean you will incur a cost for holding the position.
